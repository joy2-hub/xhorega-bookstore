Looking Back at 2023: What Were the Most Memorable Books I Encountered?

Welcome to another talk! And Happy New Year! I hope you had a lovely holiday season and the best New Years Eve. I am wishing you the absolute best 2024, filled with love, success, tons of amazing books, and lots of cozy places to read them in. I am so glad 2023 is over. It was truly one of the hardest years of my life, and I am looking forward to starting fresh. I did read a lot of great books in 2023, though, so I am excited to share those and also see which books you loved the most! I am not counting re-reads and my list is not in any special order because it is hard enough to pick 10 favorites. But here they are:

1. The Diamond Eye by Kate Quinn 2. My Phony Valentine by Courtney Walsh
3. First to Fall by Jenny B. Jones
4. The Last Bookshop in London by Madeline Martin
5. In the Weeds by B.K. Borison
6. The Only One Left by Riley Sager
7. Fourth Wing by Rebecca Yarros
8. Iron Flame by Rebecca Yarros
9. Holiday Romance by Catherine Walsh
10. The Christmas Dress by Courtney Cole

What is your absolute favorite book you read in 2023? If you can not pick, tell me several! Do we have any in common? Or do you want to read any of these?

10 January 2024

Five Books I'd Give a Different Title

This week's topic is seriously tough! Haha, sometimes I wonder what I'm even thinking when I come up with these prompts! I've gotta admit, there are plenty of titles out there that aren't my thing, but I've only managed to brainstorm new names for a few. It took me longer than I thought, but it was kinda fun getting creative... even if I could only come up with 5. So here they are:
1. Alex, Approximately by Jenn Bennett to I Wanted It to Be You
Alex, Approximately has always bothered me as a title. I never figured out where the “approximately” fit into the story. Since the book is a little bit You have Got Mail, why not title it Meg Ryan is famous quote, “I wanted it to be you.” That would have been cute!
2. A Court of Mist and Fury by Sarah J. Maas to The Stars Who Listen
I HATE the “A ___ of ___ and ___” titles. They are so overused and unoriginal. But this alternate title that is a piece of a quote Rhysand says to Feyre is thought-provoking and interesting. It also mirrors the dreaminess of the city of Velaris.
3. The Crown is Game by Evelyn Skye to The Two Enchanters
I feel too many Hunger Games vibes from this title, and eh. I just like the feel of my alternate title more. Vika and Nikolai are the only two enchanters, and must fight to become the Imperial Enchanter. The loser dies. My title just seems more intriguing! I like the word “enchanter”.
4. Dangerous Girls by Abigail Haas to Toxic
This title makes the book sound juvenile and “girly”. But the one word that can sum up the entire books is “toxic”. I really like one-word titles.
5. Hunted by Meagan Spooner to Winter Forest
This book is title tells you nothing. And you certainly have no idea the book is a lovely Beauty and the Beast retelling. My title just sounds more magical!
